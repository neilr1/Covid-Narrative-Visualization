New Zealand Daily Covid Cases and Deaths

In this narrative visualization, we explore three different countries and their experiences with COVID. As our first country, we have the United States of America. A country with great technology and medicinal capabilities, but a country that really struggled to deal with the covid pandemic. With such a large population and many people failing to adhere to the mask wearing principles until late, what were the effects of such an attitude towards COVID? Similarly what were the attitudes in a mid level sized country in Europe such as Italy where COVID cause big issues as well. Finally, we take a look at the effects that COVID had on New Zealand: a country who's government took immediate action to have mask mandates and limit entry to their country to limit both covid cases and deaths. In particular, you will find that USA had a poor reaction to covid that lead to cases and deaths rapidly increasing until vaccines started to be rolled out to communities and medical workers as well as mask mandates being more heavily enforced. In Italy, the people seemed to have taken time to listen to the mandates, although it did take them less time than in the US. In contrast, New Zealand's maximum number of cases were at the beginning of the covid cycle and through immediate action by their government to set up COVID mandates and limitations, they were able to control COVID within their country most effectively.
